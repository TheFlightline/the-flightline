## Background facts (each must trace to source)

### The deployment
- More than 380 tons of concrete from the Pensacola Bay Fishing Bridge
- 46 pieces of concrete deployed
- Bridge was damaged during Hurricane Sally in 2020
- Deployed by: Escambia County Marine Resources Division, Engineering Department, and Russell Marine, LLC
- Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission observer present to document
  → confirmed in: press release

### Reef location
- Site: Escambia County's Nearshore East Artificial Reef Site
- Coordinates: 30°17.790' N, 087°13.475' W
- Distance: 5 nautical miles southeast of Pensacola Pass
- Water depth: 43 feet
- Vertical relief: ~8 feet
  → confirmed in: press release

### Marine biology
- "Complex shapes" of concrete provide habitat for snapper, grouper, and triggerfish
- Site also serves as important spawning site for Gulf flounder
  → confirmed in: press release
