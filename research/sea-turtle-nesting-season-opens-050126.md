## Background facts (each must trace to source)

### Program details
- Escambia County Sea Turtle Conservation Program volunteers conducted first patrol the morning of May 1, 2026
- Patrols cover Pensacola Beach and Perdido Key
- Volunteers begin at first light to spot tracks, nests, and turtle activity before footprints, wind, and waves obscure tracks
- Volunteers and staff monitor nests through incubation period; remain on standby for strandings/incidents
  → confirmed in: press release

### Season length
- Nesting season runs through September
- Late-season nests can hatch as late as October
- Female turtles nest several times in a single season
- Eggs incubate ~60 days
- Hatchlings emerge "all at once under cover of darkness"
- After several years, juveniles return to area to mature
  → confirmed in: press release

### Species
- Four species nest in Escambia County
- Most common: loggerhead and green sea turtles
- Less common: Kemp's ridley and leatherback
  → confirmed in: press release

### Other coastal wildlife mentioned
- Shorebirds also nest on local beaches in summer
- Species include: least tern, snowy plover, Wilson's plover, black skimmer
- Shorebird nests are shallow scrapes in the sand with camouflaged eggs
- Eggs incubate up to 30 days; chicks are "cotton ball-like"
  → confirmed in: press release

### Public guidance ("Help Protect Our Coastal Wildlife")
1. Lights Out: bright lights disturb nesting turtles and disorient hatchlings; use red flashlight or none; turn off exterior lights and shut blinds after sunset
2. Leave Only Footprints: remove all personal items by sunset (chairs, tents, umbrellas, toys, water equipment); fill in holes; flatten sandcastles
3. Share the Beach: stay out of posted nesting areas; avoid disturbing resting birds; don't handle eggs/hatchlings; dogs only in designated dog beach parks and always on leash
  → confirmed in: press release
