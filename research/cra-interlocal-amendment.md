## Background facts

- The 2016 base interlocal between the City and CRA established the
  original framework.
  → confirmed in: 2016 Interlocal Agreement attachment to the April 23 packet
- Amendment No. 1 was adopted in 2019 and shifted day-to-day oversight
  of CRA staff into the mayor's office.
  → confirmed in: 2019 Amendment to 2016 Interlocal Agreement attachment
    to the April 23 packet
- Florida law treats community redevelopment agencies as separate legal
  entities from the municipalities that create them.
  → confirmed in: Florida Statute 163.340 (attached to the April 23 packet)
- In Pensacola the seven members of the City Council also sit as the
  CRA Board in a different legal capacity.
  → confirmed in: Pensacola CRA charter (Resolution 55-80, amended by
    Resolution 22-10), referenced on the city CRA page at
    https://www.pensacolaforward.com/cra/community-redevelopment/
- CRA Administrator Victoria D'Angelo
  → confirmed in: previous Flightline coverage and CRA staff page;
    no contradiction from primary sources reviewed
- CRA Chair: Council President Jared Moore (concurrent role)
  → confirmed in: City Council roster + CRA charter (council president
    serves as CRA Chair)
- Three CRA districts: Urban Core, Eastside, Westside
  → confirmed in: city CRA page

## Body claims that DO NOT appear in primary sources (DO NOT WRITE)

- "Cleaner year-end reconciliation between the two entities' books" —
  this language was in the original (incorrect) article and was not
  in the memo Summary. Cut from corrected version.
- "Routine cost-allocation update" — original article framing. The
  memo describes a substantive change to the staff supervision chain,
  not a routine cost-allocation update. Cut from corrected version.
- "Companion action at the April 6 CRA meeting" — original article
  asserted this. No record of CRA board action on a companion item
  on April 6 was found in the CRA agenda or minutes for that date.
  Cut from corrected version.
- Specific quotes from any council member during the 42-minute
  discussion — not transcribed in the corrected article. The
  recording exists but no individual quotes were extracted; the
  corrected article therefore characterizes positions in the
  aggregate rather than naming specific arguments to specific members.
- The 2019 sponsor of Amendment No. 1 was identified in the design
  spec for fixes 1-3 as Jewel Cannada-Wynn, but this was NOT verified
  against the 2019 council record before publication and is therefore
  NOT included in the corrected article body.
