# Correction: cra-interlocal-amendment

**Article:** Pensacola Extends Administrative Services Agreement Between City and Community Redevelopment Agency (original headline)
**Original publish date:** April 24, 2026
**Correction date:** April 30, 2026
**Slug:** cra-interlocal-amendment

## What was wrong

The original article reported that the Pensacola City Council approved Amendment No. 2 to the City-CRA interlocal agreement on April 23, 2026, "without discussion," and characterized the change as a routine update to cost-allocation language between the city and the CRA.

The April 23 City Council Regular Meeting record shows the council in fact REJECTED Amendment No. 2 by a 5-2 vote (yes: Brahier, Bare; no: Patton, Broughton, Jones, Moore, Wiggins) following approximately 42 minutes of debate (recording timestamps 01:28:22 to 02:10:09).

The substance of the amendment was also misdescribed. The amendment, sponsored by Council Member Charles Bare under File 26-486, would have rewritten Article 2.2 of the interlocal to give the CRA Administrator sole supervisory authority over CRA staff under the CRA Chair, restoring 2016/2017 language. Under the current Amendment No. 1 (adopted August 2019), CRA staff supervision sits in the mayor's chain. The original article did not address this substantive question at all.

The original article also stated that the CRA board took companion action at the April 6 CRA meeting. No such action appears in the record.

## How it happened

The article was drafted from the agenda listing — which named Item 6 and showed the sponsor's recommendation to "adopt" — combined with prior knowledge of how city-CRA interlocal agreements typically function. The author imagined what Amendment No. 2 probably said and what the council probably did, instead of opening the memorandum (File 26-486, posted to the city's CivicClerk portal) and the vote panel for Item 6.

This violated the primary-source rule that governs all Flightline reporting and skipped the pre-publish fact verification checklist.

## What was changed

The article body was rewritten end-to-end from the primary documents. The headline, dek, brief, and body now reflect the actual outcome (rejected 5-2), the actual sponsor (Bare), the actual subject matter (CRA staff supervision chain), and the actual debate length. A visible correction notice was added to the top of the article.

The slug and URL were preserved.
